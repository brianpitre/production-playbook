Introduction

Welcome to the Production Team

Every weekend, thousands of people walk through the doors of Life Church looking for something real. What they experience — the moment the lights shift, the music lands, the message connects — doesn't happen by accident. It happens because a skilled, committed production team shows up prepared, serves with excellence, and stays invisible in all the right ways.

That's the role you play. Production doesn't draw attention to itself. It creates the conditions for something greater to happen. When your work is done well, people encounter God without distraction. When it isn't, they notice the technology instead of the moment.

This playbook is your guide. It covers who we are, how we operate, what each position requires, and the specific steps you need to follow every single week. Read it carefully, refer back to it often, and bring this standard every time you serve.

Foundation

Vision, Purpose & Goal

Vision We are one church in many locations — dynamic, Spirit-filled and diverse — serving people, developing leaders and impacting generations.
Purpose Our purpose is to leverage every tool at our disposal — video, lighting, graphics, time-keeping, and more — to serve the vision of the church by creating a dynamic environment where people can have a genuine experience with God.
Goal Our goal is to consistently produce excellent services and events that are free from distraction, while continuously growing, equipping, and empowering the volunteers who make it happen.
Standards

Key Attributes of a Production Team Member

Technical skill matters — but it's secondary to character. These are the qualities that define every strong member of this team.

Detail-Oriented You notice what others miss. You catch problems before they become distractions and correct them without being asked.
Punctual You are at your position, ready, at your call time — not walking in at your call time. The team depends on you being where you need to be.
Available You show up for your scheduled services and are willing to serve for special events when needed. Reliability is part of the role.
Decisive You execute confidently — both the things you've rehearsed and the things you haven't. When the plan changes, you adapt and move forward without hesitation.
Flexible Services change. Elements shift. You adapt quickly and calmly without frustration, and you communicate changes clearly to the rest of the team.
Patient You stay composed and attentive from the first moment to the last. Fatigue, stress, and long mornings don't show up in your work.
Teachable You receive feedback without defensiveness and actively look for ways to improve. Your best work is always in front of you, not behind you.
Team-First You serve the mission over personal preference. You communicate proactively, cover gaps, and never leave a teammate hanging.
How We Operate

Team Overview

Life Church operates a two-model production structure across its campuses. The Houston Levee campus serves as the Broadcast Location — it originates the live service, running a full broadcast production that is distributed via Vitec (our video distribution system) to four Receiving Locations in real time.

Each of the four Receiving Locations has its own production team responsible for seamlessly integrating the incoming Vitec feed while managing local production elements — cameras, slides, and any on-site content.

This means the two campuses operate similarly but carry different weights of responsibility. Houston Levee controls what every campus sees. Receiving Locations ensure their local experience matches the broadcast's quality and intention.

Broadcast Location — Houston Levee Originates the live program feed. Runs full production including all cameras, graphics, video playback, and the outgoing Vitec signal. The Broadcast Director monitors and manages the outbound feed.
Receiving Locations (4 campuses) Receive the live Vitec feed and integrate it with local production. Manage on-site cameras for in-room screens, local slides, and attend the Tech Touchdown before each service to align on flow and transitions.

Throughout this playbook, differences between Broadcast and Receiving responsibilities are noted clearly in each section.

Roles

Production Positions & Responsibilities

Director

The Director owns the visual presentation of the service. Working from the production booth, the Director calls every camera shot, communicates transitions to the Tech Director, and ensures what goes to screen is engaging, focused, and distraction-free. This role requires deep preparation — the Director should know the service better than anyone in the booth.

  • Study the service flow and worship setlist in advance of each service
  • Call all camera shots clearly and confidently — by number and frame description
  • Communicate transitions, segment changes, and cues to the Tech Director and Camera Operators
  • Monitor pacing, framing, and composition on every shot going to program
  • Respond immediately to changes communicated by the 5 Star or other team members
  • Lead camera rehearsal during the Full Service Run-through
  • Attend Touchdown and confirm any last-minute changes
Houston Levee Directs all local cameras and coordinates with the Broadcast Director to ensure the outgoing program feed is broadcast-appropriate at every moment.
Receiving Locations Monitors the incoming Vitec feed and directs local cameras for in-room screens. Attends Tech Touchdown to align on Vitec/local transition points.

Tech Director

The Tech Director operates the video switcher, executing the Director's shot calls in real time. This role demands precision, fast reflexes, and total familiarity with the switcher layout. Every cut, dissolve, and transition is a live, unforgiving action — there are no retakes.

  • Power on and verify all switcher inputs and outputs before rehearsal
  • Execute cuts, dissolves, and wipes exactly as called by the Director
  • Monitor all camera feeds and source inputs on the multiviewer continuously
  • Manage video playback switching between live cameras and pre-recorded content
  • Be prepared to go to a backup source immediately if a feed is lost
  • Communicate any signal issues to the Director right away
Houston Levee Manages the program feed that routes to the Vitec distribution system. Coordinates with the Broadcast Director on transition timing and outbound signal quality.

Camera Operator

Camera Operators capture the people and moments that make the service compelling on screen. This role demands technical precision — clean focus, smooth movements, proper framing — and the instinct to have the right shot ready before the Director calls it.

  • Set white balance, exposure, and focus before rehearsal begins
  • Execute pan, tilt, zoom, and focus with smooth, deliberate movements
  • "Sell" shots to the Director — frame confidently and hold steady until called
  • Respond to Director's shot calls instantly and without hesitation
  • Study the setlist and service flow to anticipate movements and key moments
  • Communicate to the Director immediately if a shot is unavailable or a camera issue arises

ProPresenter Operator

The ProPresenter Operator controls all on-screen text and media — lyrics, scriptures, announcement graphics, and video roll-ins. This role requires thorough advance preparation. A missed lyric or late slide break's the congregation's focus instantly and is one of the most visible errors in a service.

  • Load the Sunday service playlist and verify all slides and videos before rehearsal
  • Follow all songs during Worship Team rehearsal — know every verse, chorus, and bridge
  • Lead lyrics consistently — advance by half a line, never lag behind the congregation
  • Fire videos and advance slides precisely on cue
  • Communicate immediately if a slide is missing, incorrect, or a video fails to load
  • Know the Clear All function and use it without hesitation if wrong content goes live

PVP Operator

The PVP (ProVideoPlayer) Operator manages all motion background and environmental video content throughout the service. This position works in close coordination with the ProPresenter Operator and the Tech Director to ensure backgrounds are correct, transitions are clean, and every clip is cued and ready.

  • Load all playlists and verify all clips are present and in the correct order
  • Test playback quality, color, and audio levels for every clip before rehearsal
  • Confirm PVP output is routing correctly to the switcher
  • Coordinate with the ProPresenter Operator on shared timing and transitions
  • Respond to Director and Tech Director cues in real time
  • Adjust hue, brightness, or speed during playback as needed

Time Clock Operator

The Time Clock Operator manages service timing, keeping the production team informed of where the service stands against the planned flow at every moment. This role is critical to preventing overruns and ensuring smooth, on-time transitions between services.

  • Review all time segments in the service flow before the run-through
  • Align countdown segments to match the service flow sequence
  • Track actual vs. planned timing during run-through; document all discrepancies
  • Communicate timing information to the Director and 5 Star proactively
  • Alert the Director if any segment is running long or short
  • Adjust clock cues on the fly as the service evolves

Broadcast Director

The Broadcast Director is a Houston Levee-specific role responsible for the outgoing program feed distributed via Vitec to all four Receiving Locations. Working alongside the Director, the Broadcast Director ensures the feed is broadcast-quality, properly framed for a distributed audience, and technically stable at all times.

  • Review the full service flow with a broadcast lens — identify every Vitec transition point
  • Confirm Vitec distribution is online and feeding all Receiving Locations before service
  • Continuously monitor the outgoing program feed — not just in-room content
  • Coordinate with the Tech Director on transition timing for broadcast-appropriate cuts
  • Ensure all pre-recorded content is broadcast-quality before it airs
  • Communicate feed status to Receiving Locations if technical issues arise
Houston Levee Only This role does not exist at Receiving Locations. Receiving Locations have a Producer/Assistant Producer who manages local production decisions.

Recording Engineer

The Recording Engineer captures all video feeds from the service for post-production use — including message editing and content distribution. This role requires meticulous attention to signal quality and disciplined file management after every service.

  • Set up all recording equipment and verify signal routing before call time
  • Insert and verify recording media (SSD or equivalent) before service begins
  • Test record and verify audio and video quality before the run-through
  • Monitor recording levels and signal throughout the entire service
  • Properly eject, label, and back up all media immediately after service
  • Verify file integrity before leaving; deliver edited content per agreed timeline
Onboarding

Training Process

Training happens at each location during weekend services through a structured shadowing model. The timeline adjusts based on the individual's prior experience, but the progression does not skip steps. No one moves to a rostered position without completing the full training process and meeting with the Team Leader.

Broadcast Location — Houston Levee

PhaseFocusDescription
Weeks 1–2 I Do. You Watch. Observe the position in full. Take notes. Ask questions after the service — not during.
Week 3 Campus Visit Observe at a Receiving Location. Understand what they receive via Vitec and what they need from the broadcast campus to do their job well.
Week 4 You Do. I Help. Take the position with the trainer alongside you. The Team Leader evaluates readiness and team fit at this stage.
Weeks 5–6 You Do. I Watch. Operate fully independently. Trainer is present but hands off entirely unless an issue arises.

Receiving Locations

PhaseFocusDescription
Weeks 1–2 I Do. You Watch. Observe the position in full. Understand the Vitec integration, local camera responsibilities, and Touchdown process.
Week 3 You Do. I Help. Take the position with the trainer alongside you. The Team Leader evaluates readiness and team fit.
Weeks 4–5 You Do. I Watch. Operate fully independently. Trainer is present but hands off.
Before training is considered complete, every team member must meet with the Team Leader for a brief evaluation conversation.

Checklists

Position Checklists

Use these checklists every week — not just during training. Consistency is what separates good production from great production.

Director Checklist

Preservice

Before Full Service Run-Through

  • Review the service flow document thoroughly
  • Listen to all worship setlist songs — note key moments, intros, tag endings, and transitions
  • Note any special elements, video transitions, or Vitec segments
  • Confirm camera assignments and positions with all Camera Operators
  • Verify intercom (comms) is functioning — test with all positions
  • Confirm all cameras are powered on and feeding signal to the switcher

During Full Service Run-Through

Goal: Rehearse every segment so the first service is excellent. Call every shot. Communicate every transition.

  • Worship — Call camera shots from downbeat to close Identify 2–3 go-to shots for each song (wide worship, singer close, band wide, etc.) Watch transitions between songs — confirm PVP/lighting cues match camera transitions Note builds and tag endings to anticipate camera moves in advance
  • MC Segments — Confirm blocking and camera coverage for each MC Identify which camera handles each MC position; call graphics transitions with Tech Director
  • Special Segments — Rehearse any unique or non-standard elements
  • Message Segment — Confirm camera positions; note any screen content or slide transitions Houston Levee: confirm Vitec transition points with Broadcast Director Receiving Locations: confirm Vitec feed timing and local camera plan

After Full Service Run-Through

  • Communicate any updates, corrections, or changes to Tech Director and Camera Operators
  • Note any camera positions that need adjustment before first service
Touchdown

Goal: Confirm all teams are aligned on service details and backup scenarios.

Houston Levee Touchdown only needed if changes occurred after the run-through. Communicate any changes to the Tech Director before the service.
Receiving Locations Attend Tech Touchdown. Hear the flow, transitions, and special elements from the 5 Star. Ask any questions about Vitec transitions, local segment timing, and slide content before leaving.
Service
  • Welcome — Call opening shots; confirm camera is live and framed before countdown reaches 0
  • Ministry Time — Monitor stage; call appropriate camera coverage through the full worship set
  • Close Worship / Info — Transition cameras cleanly from worship to appropriate coverage
  • Offering — Confirm graphic/slide transitions with Tech Director on cue
  • Meet and Greet — Use this window for a brief camera reset; confirm positions for next segment
  • Videos — Direct Tech Director to program feed for video; hold until video ends, then cut back to stage
  • Message — Execute message camera plan; stay alert to speaker movement and positioning Communicate any slide-related transitions to Tech Director
  • Close — Call final shots; confirm with Tech Director when to transition to close graphic or countdown
Post Service
  • Debrief with Camera Operators and Tech Director — what worked, what needs adjustment
  • Log any technical issues or shot changes for reference
  • Discuss tweaks with Production Team leaders before the next service

Camera Operator Checklist

Preservice

Before Full Service Run-Through

  • Arrive at call time; power on camera and remove lens cap immediately
  • Verify camera is properly mounted and secured on the tripod head
  • Connect camera cable; confirm signal is reaching the production booth
  • Set white balance for current stage lighting conditions
  • Set exposure and gain; avoid blown highlights on faces
  • Test zoom range and confirm focus is smooth and accurate
  • Test pan and tilt — confirm head is balanced and movements are fluid
  • Confirm intercom (comms) is live and Director is clearly audible
  • Review your assigned camera position and primary shot responsibilities

During Full Service Run-Through

Goal: Rehearse your shots so every move is intentional and confident. Sell every frame to the Director.

  • Worship — Find and hold your assigned shots; practice transitions between songs Develop 2–3 reliable go-to shots per song format (wide, close, band) Practice zoom moves on song builds and tag endings
  • MC Segments — Frame your assigned MC; confirm blocking and coverage with Director
  • Special Segments — Rehearse any non-standard camera coverage
  • Message — Confirm message framing; practice reframing for speaker movement

After Full Service Run-Through

  • Communicate any issues (focus, cable tension, obstructed sightlines) to the Director
  • Adjust camera position if Director requests a change before first service
Touchdown
  • Listen for any camera coverage changes; ask if anything is unclear
Service
  • Welcome — On position; hold opening frame steady until called
  • Ministry Time — Execute Director's calls; stay locked in on your zone
  • Close Worship / Info — Smooth transitions; anticipate song endings
  • Offering — Hold or park on a wide, safe frame
  • Meet and Greet — Reframe if needed for next segment; stay ready
  • Videos — Camera is typically not live; reposition for next segment if needed
  • Message — Track speaker movement; maintain proper framing and headroom at all times
  • Close — Hold final frame until released by Director
Post Service
  • Power down camera if instructed between services
  • Report any equipment issues (focus, cable, head movement) to the Team Leader
  • Debrief with Director on any shot adjustments for next service

Recording Engineer Checklist

Preservice

Before Full Service Run-Through

  • Power on all recording equipment and verify all connections
  • Insert fresh recording media (SSD or equivalent) — never reuse media without confirming it has been cleared
  • Verify the recording device is receiving the correct program feed (confirm with Tech Director or Broadcast Director)
  • Set and verify recording levels — avoid clipping; confirm clean signal
  • Test record for 30 seconds; play back to verify both video and audio quality
  • Confirm file naming convention with Team Leader Standard format: Date_Service_Location (e.g., 2026-03-16_9AM_HoustonLevee)
  • Confirm intercom (comms) is live

During Full Service Run-Through

  • Monitor recording signal to confirm feed is correct and stable
  • Note any signal issues or feed changes that may affect recording quality

After Full Service Run-Through

  • Confirm recording system is armed and ready for first service
  • Communicate any technical issues to the Team Leader
Touchdown
  • Confirm with Broadcast Director or Tech Director if any recording source changes are planned
Service
  • Before countdown — Begin recording before the countdown video starts; never start late
  • Welcome through Message — Monitor recording levels and feed quality throughout Watch for signal loss, dropped frames, or disk errors Do not stop recording mid-service unless directed by the Team Leader
  • Close — Continue recording through close and any post-service content as required
  • Stop recording — Stop at the designated point after service (confirm endpoint with Team Leader)
Post Service
  • Allow recording media to finalize safely — never force eject
  • Eject recording media properly; label immediately with date, service time, and location
  • Back up all recorded files to designated storage location
  • Verify file integrity — confirm video plays back correctly before leaving
  • Deliver any edited message content per the agreed format and timeline
  • Report any recording issues to the Team Leader
